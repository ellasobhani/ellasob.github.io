Patient Portal for Nontraditional Students

For this project, our team focused on redesigning the Williams College health portal to better serve nontraditional students, including veterans, students with families, and older students. The original interface was cluttered, text-heavy, and confusing, even for traditional users. Our goal was to create a simplified, intuitive experience grounded in accessibility, clarity, and user needs for our user group.

Through needfinding and usability testing, we identified key pain points in the current design: unclear navigation, redundant pages, overwhelming instructions, and a lack of tailored resources. We restructured the portal around three core sections—Required Forms, Athlete Resources, and Nontraditional Student Resources—and used smart form filtering based on student profiles to eliminate unnecessary content for each user. Our visual design emphasized clear hierarchy, contrasting colors, and consistent UI elements, particularly for mobile users and those with limited tech experience.

To improve usability, we added a collapsible sidebar menu, made forms easy to access and track, and created dedicated resource pages with relevant information. We also used color-coded cues (red = incomplete, green = complete) and implemented reusable Figma components for consistency. The result is a cleaner, faster, and more accessible health portal, especially for nontraditional students whose needs are currently almost impossible to be met by the existing portal.
