Online Book Catalog

For this project we focused on transforming a barebones, only partially functional, online book catalog into a user-friendly, visually engaging platform. We began by identifying usability issues in the original site—such as the lack of persistent selection, missing book details, poor visual hierarchy, and ineffective controls—and sought to improve both navigation and decision-making features.

Key updates included adding sortable filters (by title, author, year), book cover images, and hover-based summaries to streamline browsing. We applied human-centered design principles like visual hierarchy, contrast, and clear affordances (hover states, signifiers) to guide users intuitively. Aesthetic changes, inspired by local libraries and bookstores, gave the site a warmer, more inviting tone.

User feedback was pretty central throughout our entire process. We conducted interviews, created empathy maps, and performed usability testing. We added features like genre filters, star ratings, and responsive layout. Our evaluation by participants confirmed that our redesign had major improvements in functionality, clarity, and appeal.
