Gesture Recognition for Touch-Free Gaming

This project reimagines digital interaction through touch-free gesture recognition, allowing users to control games using only hand movements. Our team designed a gesture-based interface that replaces traditional keyboard inputs with intuitive hand gestures. We began by analyzing a handful of browser-based games we were familiar with that could be suitable for our task. We went brainstormed and went through the ideation process for Super Stacker 2, Fireboy & Watergirl, and Bloxorz. After our ideation process we did Wizard-of-Oz testing to test our gestures and determined which game would be most appropriate to move forward with. We ended up selecting Braininess 2, a puzzle game that allows the user to jump from space block to space block, eliminating each block they leave behind. The game’s reliance on deliberate, sequential actions made it an ideal candidate for gesture-based controls. Using MediaPipe’s Hand Landmarks API, we developed a custom gesture recognition system to simulate keyboard input via PyAutoGUI. Our implementation supports: directional movement (thumbs up/down, index finger point, directional thumb swipes), custom gestures for gameplay control, and a “rest state” gesture for intentional pacing between moves. The final prototype successfully enables players to complete all Braininess 2 levels without touching a keyboard or mouse.
